Our Projects

Targeted initiatives driving real, measurable policy change across America's most critical challenges.

Cities

Gotham Renaissance

The Gotham Renaissance project is the Manhattan Institute's flagship urban policy initiative, dedicated to restoring New York City as a beacon of innovation, opportunity, and quality of life. Through research, public advocacy, and policy engagement, the project addresses the most pressing challenges facing America's largest city.

Key focus areas include housing affordability and zoning reform, transportation and infrastructure modernization, public safety and quality-of-life policing, economic development and fiscal responsibility, and education reform.

Economics

Proxy Monitor

Proxy Monitor is the first and only publicly available database tracking shareholder proposals and proxy voting at America's largest corporations. Launched in 2011, the project provides transparency into the corporate governance process and sheds light on how activist shareholder proposals affect businesses and investors.

The project's annual reports have become essential reading for corporate boards, institutional investors, regulators, and policymakers. By making proxy data accessible and analyzable, Proxy Monitor serves as a vital check on the growing influence of ESG-oriented shareholder activism.

Trial Lawyers, Inc.

Trial Lawyers, Inc. is the Manhattan Institute's signature legal reform project, examining the vast and often hidden costs of America's litigation industry. Through annual reports, policy briefs, and public engagement, the project documents how excessive litigation burdens businesses, raises costs for consumers, and distorts public policy.

Since its launch, Trial Lawyers, Inc. has published annual reports examining different facets of the litigation industry, from asbestos litigation and mass torts to patent trolling and government lawsuits. The project's work has informed legislative reform efforts at both the state and federal levels.

Culture

The Logos Initiative

The Logos Initiative is dedicated to defending institutional integrity and promoting the free exchange of ideas in American life. In an era of growing ideological conformity, the initiative provides research, analysis, and advocacy to protect intellectual diversity in academia, media, government, and the private sector.

The initiative draws its name from the Greek word for reason, word, and principle, reflecting its commitment to rational discourse and the open pursuit of truth. Through reports, commentary, and public events, the Logos Initiative challenges ideological capture and champions institutional reform.

Economics

Adam Smith Society

The Adam Smith Society is a network of MBA students and business professionals committed to promoting the intellectual foundations of free enterprise. With chapters at leading business schools across the country, the Society provides a forum for discussing the moral, economic, and practical case for capitalism.

Through campus events, national conferences, mentorship programs, and publications, the Adam Smith Society cultivates a new generation of business leaders who understand and can articulate the principles that make free markets work. The Society's annual meeting brings together hundreds of members for panels, debates, and networking.

Education

Higher Education Reform

The Manhattan Institute's Higher Education Reform initiative advances accountability, transparency, and academic freedom in America's colleges and universities. As tuition costs soar and concerns about ideological conformity grow, MI scholars produce cutting-edge research on how to improve outcomes for students and restore public trust in higher education.

Key areas of focus include federal student-loan reform and income-share agreements, transparency in university spending and outcomes data, protecting intellectual diversity and academic freedom on campus, and alternative credentialing and workforce development pathways.
