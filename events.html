Alexander Hamilton Award Dinner

The Manhattan Institute's most prestigious event, honoring individuals who have made extraordinary contributions to American public life and embody the spirit of Alexander Hamilton's vision for a prosperous, dynamic nation. Black tie. The Cipriani, 25 Broadway, New York City.

Distinguished Lecture

The Hayek Lecture

Named in honor of Nobel laureate Friedrich A. Hayek, this annual lecture explores the principles of free markets, individual liberty, and the dangers of central planning. The lecture brings together leading economists, policymakers, and thought leaders. Manhattan Institute, 52 Vanderbilt Avenue, New York.

Annual Lecture

James Q. Wilson Lecture on Character and Culture

Honoring the legacy of the renowned political scientist James Q. Wilson, this lecture examines the relationship between character, culture, and public policy. The series addresses how civic virtues and institutional norms shape American life. Manhattan Institute, New York.

The Future of Urban Policing

A panel discussion on evidence-based approaches to public safety, featuring leading scholars and practitioners. Topics include proactive policing strategies, community engagement, and the role of technology in law enforcement. Manhattan Institute, New York.
